# Stage 7 P1 Single-Reviewer Pilot Implementation Plan

> **For agentic workers:** REQUIRED SUB-SKILL: Use superpowers:subagent-driven-development (recommended) or superpowers:executing-plans to implement this plan task-by-task. Steps use checkbox (`- [ ]`) syntax for tracking.

**Goal:** Complete six source-bound human review outcomes, beginning with `house-a-small-modern-house`, while measuring a repeatable single-reviewer workflow that can inform the later 20-case and 2000-case campaigns.

**Architecture:** Process exactly one case at a time through source evidence, human governance decisions, orientation review, semantic review, append-only overlay validation, Dataset v2 rebuild, and readiness reporting. Automation prepares evidence and validates records; the human reviewer alone supplies identity and final conclusions.

**Tech Stack:** Node.js 20+, Stage 7 review-pack and dataset CLIs, JSONL append-only review overlay, PowerShell, SHA-256, public web source and license evidence.

## Global Constraints

- The canonical review overlay is `mc_templates/curation/stage7_dataset_reviews.jsonl`.
- Every record is bound to the exact source SHA-256 from its review pack.
- Automation must not invent a reviewer, author, license conclusion, permission, canonical front side, approved learning area, or semantic acceptance.
- Unknown or ambiguous training permission remains fail-closed and training-ineligible.
- A source change invalidates the old review rather than transferring it.
- Only explicit sparse `set` or `clear` corrections with human reasons are allowed.
- Dataset v1 remains immutable.
- Dataset v2 is rebuilt after every appended review record.
- No real-data training starts until six explicit outcomes, three semantic accepts, three eligible cases, and a zero readiness-gate exit code exist.
- The first case is `house-a-small-modern-house` with source SHA-256 `e602703d076cfa70903c10925f4dddd1000109ab1afacb46a57368461c9b4b55`.
- The current split assignment for the case remains authoritative and must not be edited by review.

### Task 2: Collect Source, Author, and License Evidence

**Files:**
- Read: the source URL and directly linked author, terms, license, and download pages.
- Do not modify repository files during evidence collection.

**Interfaces:**
- Consumes: public pages directly attributable to the source host or named author.
- Produces: a cited evidence summary that distinguishes observed facts, inference, and unresolved questions.

- [ ] **Step 1: Inspect the exact source page**

Open `https://mcbuild.org/schematics/16786:a-small-modern-house` and record only facts shown on that page: title, uploader or author, publication date if shown, download identity, and any license or usage statement.

Expected: every recorded fact has a direct page citation or remains explicitly unknown.

- [ ] **Step 2: Inspect the site's authoritative terms and license pages**

Follow source-page links or same-site navigation to terms of service, copyright, licensing, download conditions, and user-upload rules. Do not treat search-result snippets, third-party reposts, or absence of a prohibition as training permission.

Expected: exact URLs and a concise paraphrase of the clauses relevant to local analysis, local training, derived metadata, and public release.

- [ ] **Step 3: Inspect the named author or uploader evidence**

If the source page names an author or uploader, inspect the linked profile and any explicit license statement for this artifact. If uploader and original author differ or cannot be distinguished, record that ambiguity.

Expected: author identity is either directly evidenced or recorded as unknown; it is never inferred from the filename.

- [ ] **Step 4: Present a fail-closed governance recommendation to the human reviewer**

The recommendation must separately address:

```text
license_status: verified | restricted | prohibited | unknown
allowed_uses: local-analysis | local-training | derived-metadata | public-release
public release of raw source: yes | no | unknown
evidence strength: direct artifact license | site-wide terms | uploader statement | unresolved
```

Expected: `local-training` is recommended only when affirmative evidence supports it.

### Task 3: Review Orientation and Semantic Learning Areas

**Files:**
- Read: source-page screenshots or previews.
- Read: `.tmp/stage7-p1/house-a-small-modern-house/cases/house-a-small-modern-house/plan.raw.json`
- Read: `.tmp/stage7-p1/house-a-small-modern-house/cases/house-a-small-modern-house/report.md`

**Interfaces:**
- Consumes: visible source evidence and extracted semantic diagnostics.
- Produces: a human-confirmed front side, explicit decisions for all three learning areas, and a semantic outcome.

- [ ] **Step 1: Identify the canonical front from visible evidence**

Compare entrance, primary facade, approach, and opening hierarchy across available source previews. Map the visible front to the normalized `north|south|east|west` transform. If the source previews do not establish the transform, keep `canonical_front_side` null and the case non-positive.

- [ ] **Step 2: Decide each learning area independently**

For `envelope`, `site`, and `space`, record approve or block based on whether the source and extraction provide reliable learnable evidence. Do not approve a layer solely because it contains non-empty cells.

- [ ] **Step 3: Decide semantic acceptance**

Current extraction blockers are:

```text
missing-circulation
missing-entrance
missing-floor-continuity
missing-roof
missing-usable-space
missing-vertical-circulation
```

Accept only if visible evidence and sparse, auditable corrections can resolve the hard blockers without relabeling broad regions. Otherwise retain semantic rejection and record the reason.

- [ ] **Step 4: Present one complete proposed outcome to the human reviewer**

Expected: the proposal includes reviewer-neutral evidence, recommended status, front side, all layer decisions, zero or more exact sparse corrections, and explicit remaining uncertainty. No overlay record is written at this step.
